JEE Main 2026 — Marks vs Percentile Explained

Published July 21, 2026 · Session 1 & 2 combined analysis

JEE Main uses percentile-based scoring, not raw marks. Because of two sessions and multiple shifts, NTA normalizes scores. Here's how many marks you actually need for each percentile band.

🧮 How NTA Normalization Works

Because two sessions run and each has multiple shifts, raw marks can't be compared. NTA converts your raw marks in your shift into a percentile — literally, "% of candidates you scored above".

Percentile = (100 × candidates below or equal to you in your session) / total candidates in that session

Between two sessions, the best of two percentiles is your final NTA score. Attempt both sessions unless you're sure about your first attempt.

🎓 Percentile Needed for Top Colleges

  • JEE Advanced qualification: Top 2,50,000 → ~92 percentile
  • NIT Trichy CSE: 99.4+ (rank ~3,000)
  • IIIT Hyderabad CSE: 99.7+ (rank ~1,500)
  • BITS Pilani (via BITSAT — separate exam): Score 320+
  • Home-state NIT: 96–98 percentile (state quota advantage)

⚠️ Common Misconceptions

"Percentile = percentage" — WRONG. 99 percentile means 99% of students scored below you, not that you scored 99%.

"January session cutoff = April session cutoff" — Normalization ensures the same percentile carries the same weight regardless of session.
